# 3×5 Notecard Cheat Sheet (two-sided)

Goal: dense, write-small notes. This is formatted as **Side A** and **Side B**.

---

## SIDE A (Front)

### DOMAINS + CELL TYPES
- 3 domains: **Bacteria**, **Archaea**, **Eukarya**
- Prokaryotes = Bacteria + Archaea: **single-celled**, **no membrane-bound organelles**, DNA in **cytoplasm**, divide by **binary fission**
- Eukaryotes: organelles; divide by **mitosis/meiosis**; multicellularity common

### ENDOSYMBIOSIS
- **Mitochondria** = engulfed bacterium
- **Chloroplast** = engulfed **cyanobacterium**
- Evidence: own **DNA**, **fission**, own **ribosomes**

### PROKARYOTE STRUCTURES + DIVERSITY
- **Pili** (pilus): DNA transfer (conjugation)
- **Capsule**: sticky; soap+water helps remove
- Diversity/energy strategies:
  - **Autotroph** = makes food; **Heterotroph** = eats others
  - **Photoautotroph** (sun) vs **Chemoautotroph** (**chemosynthesis**)
  - Heterotroph types: **decomposer**, **pathogen**, consumer
- Prokaryote genetic diversity (no sex): **conjugation** + **pick up DNA** + **viral DNA** + **high mutation**
- **Microbiome**: gut flora benefits (digestion/immune/vitamins etc)
- **Archaea**: often extremophiles; split from bacteria due to biochemistry differences

### EUKARYOTIC DIVERSITY (non plant/animal/fungi)
- **Protozoans**: 1-celled heterotrophic euks (ex **Giardia** parasite; **amoeba** uses **pseudopods**; some cilia/flagella)
- **Algae**: photosynthetic euks **NOT plants**
  - **Phytoplankton** = single-celled photosynthetic euk
  - **Macroalgae/seaweed** = multicellular algae
- Green algae (closest to plants): pigments like plants; **starch** storage; **cellulose** walls
- Brown algae + diatoms: store **oils**; diatoms = **glass shell**; **kelp** = brown algae
  - kelp parts: **holdfast, stipe, air bladder, blades**
- Red algae: **phycobilins**; **Floridean starch**; **agar/carrageenan**
- Dinoflagellates: **flagella**; **cellulose plates**; can glow; blooms (“red tide” misnomer)
- Slime molds: euk decomposers that eat bacteria; can switch single↔multi-cell stages

## SIDE B (Back)

### FUNGI
- Fungi: euk, **heterotrophic** (NO photosynthesis)
- Feed by **external digestion** → enzymes → liquefy → absorb
- **Saprophytic** = external digestion of dead matter
- Cell wall: **chitin**
- Body: **hypha** (pl), **mycelium** (whole body)
- Dispersal: **spores**; fruiting body = spore structure
- Sex: hyphae fuse; **heterokaryotic** (2 nuclei/cell) stage; later fuse → meiosis → spores
- Symbiosis terms: **mutualism**, **parasitism**, **commensalism**
  - must-know mutualisms: **lichen** (fungus+algae), **mycorrhizae** (fungus+plant roots)

### PLANTS
- Plants: euk, multicellular, autotroph; cellulose walls; chloroplasts
- Land adaptations: **cuticle**, **stomata**, **vascular tissue**

**Alternation of generations loop**
- **Gametophyte (n)** —mitosis→ **gametes**
- fertilization → **zygote (2n)** —mitosis→ **sporophyte (2n)**
- **sporophyte** —meiosis→ **spores (n)** —mitosis→ gametophyte

Groups + key traits
- **Mosses (Bryophyta)**: no vascular; spores; sperm **swim** to egg; small
- **Ferns**: vascular; spores (sori under fronds); sperm swim
- **Pines (gymnosperms; Pinophyta mentioned)**: **seeds + pollen** in **cones**; pollen = male gametophyte (no swimming sperm)
- **Flowering plants (angiosperms)**: **flowers + fruits**

Flower parts
- Sepal, petal
- **Stamen** = filament + anther (pollen)
- **Pistil** = stigma + style + ovary (ovules→seeds; ovary→fruit)

### ANIMALIA (Part 1)
- Animals: multicellular euk; **no cell walls**; internal digestion
- Embryo: **blastula/blastocyst** → **gastrulation** → **gastrula** (digestive cavity forms)
- **Protostome vs deuterostome**: first opening becomes **mouth** vs **anus**
- Vertebrate vs invertebrate: backbone vs no backbone
- Evolution themes: radial→bilateral; cavity→tube

Phyla covered
- **Porifera** (sponges): **pores**, **osculum**, **collar cells** (flagella), **spicules**, filter feeder
- **Cnidaria**: **medusa** vs **polyp**; **radial**; **gastrovascular cavity**; **cnidocytes**
- **Platyhelminthes** (flatworms): **bilateral**, eyespots; cilia gliding; gastrovascular cavity; mouth mid-belly; regeneration; tapeworms
- **Nematodes** (roundworms): **alimentary canal** (mouth→anus); **cuticle**; cylindrical + tapered ends; parasites/free-living
- **Mollusca**: **mantle, muscular foot, gill, radula**; examples: snails/slugs, clams/mussels, octopus/squid
- **Annelida**: **segmentation**, **setae** (bristles); earthworms; marine tube/feather duster worms
- **Arthropoda**: **chitin exoskeleton**, **jointed legs**, **molting**; insects/spiders/crabs
- **Echinodermata**: adult **5-part radial**; **water vascular system**, **tube feet**, **endoskeleton**; sea stars/urchins/cucumbers
- **Chordata**: **dorsal hollow nerve cord, notochord, pharyngeal slits, post-anal tail**

### ECOLOGY
- Ecology ≠ environmentalism
- Levels: **population** (1 species), **community** (species interactions), **ecosystem** (+ abiotic)

Population growth
- Exponential = **J curve**
- Logistic = **S curve**; levels at **carrying capacity (K)**

Community interactions
- **Competition**, **predation**, **symbiosis** (mutualism/parasitism/commensalism)

Ecosystems: trophic levels + energy
- **Producers** → primary → secondary → tertiary; **decomposers** act on all levels
- **~10%** energy transfer up; **~90%** lost as heat
- Energy flows one-way (light→heat); nutrients **cycle** (water/carbon/nitrogen)
- Nitrogen: plants use **nitrates**; bacteria convert N forms into usable forms
- Disturbance → **succession** (primary on bare rock; secondary w/soil) → climax community
- **Keystone species** maintains diversity (urchin/kelp example)
